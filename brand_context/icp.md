# ICP — End User Profile

> Foundation file: WHO uses the app? Skills writing in-app copy load this to match language, knowledge level, and concerns.
>
> Distinct from `code_context/runbook.md` stakeholders (those are devs/ops). This is the **end user** of the running product.

## Primary persona

- **Name (archetype)**: _e.g. "Solo founder Sarah" / "Mid-career engineer Alex"_
- **Role**: _e.g. founder / engineer / designer / ops lead_
- **Company size**: _solo / small team (2-20) / mid (20-200) / enterprise_
- **Tech-savviness**: _novice / comfortable / power user / expert_

## What they're trying to do

_The job-to-be-done in their words._

Example: _"Ship features without breaking what's already there."_

## Their day before this app

_Concrete pain points, in their language._

- _e.g. "Reviews take 2 days because nobody has time"_
- _e.g. "I miss bugs because tests don't catch logic mistakes"_
- _e.g. "Every PR breaks something different in CI"_

## Their day with this app

_What changes once they use it._

- _e.g. "I get a sanity check before requesting review"_
- _e.g. "I catch obvious mistakes before they hit my reviewer"_

## Knowledge level

_What can we assume they know? Adjust copy accordingly._

- **Coding**: _yes — assume git, PRs, test runners_
- **Our product domain**: _new — explain in-product on first use_
- **Jargon allowed**: _e.g. PR, CI, lint — yes; e.g. monorepo, RAG — no without explanation_

## Concerns / objections

- _e.g. "Is my code being sent to a third party?"_ → addressed in privacy modal + onboarding
- _e.g. "Will this slow down my workflow?"_ → addressed in first-run experience

## Language they use

_Five words/phrases your user types in tickets or says in interviews. Skills should match this register._

- _e.g. "broken", "flaky", "shipping", "PR is stuck", "merged"_

## Anti-persona

_Who is this NOT for? Helps us cut copy that confuses the primary._

- _e.g. Non-technical product managers — not the primary; we don't target their needs_
